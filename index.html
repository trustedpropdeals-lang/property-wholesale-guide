Property Wholesaling & Rental Arbitrage Guide

Learn the fundamentals of real‑estate investing with our comprehensive guide.

What is Property Wholesaling?

Property wholesaling involves securing a property under contract at a below‑market price and then assigning that contract to another investor for a fee. This allows you to generate income without purchasing or renovating the property.

Steps to Start Wholesaling

  • Market research: Analyze local real estate trends, regulations, and comparable sales.
  • Build a buyers list: Network with cash buyers ready to purchase deals.
  • Find distressed properties: Identify undervalued properties through driving for dollars, MLS searches, or direct mail.
  • Negotiate & secure contracts: Get properties under contract with assignment clauses.
  • Assign the contract: Transfer the purchase agreement to a buyer for an assignment fee.

What is Rental Arbitrage?

Rental arbitrage involves leasing a property long‑term and subletting it on short‑term rental platforms such as Airbnb or VRBO. The goal is to profit from the difference between the long‑term rent and the higher short‑term rental income.

Steps to Start Rental Arbitrage

  • Understand local regulations: Ensure short‑term rentals are allowed and comply with permits and taxes.
  • Secure the right property: Choose properties in high‑demand areas with desirable amenities.
  • Negotiate lease terms: Ensure your lease allows subletting for short‑term rentals.
  • Calculate profit margins: Account for rent, furnishings, utilities, platform fees, and taxes.
  • Launch professionally: Create polished listings, use dynamic pricing, and provide excellent guest experiences.

Tips and Considerations

  • Consult a real‑estate attorney to ensure your contracts are compliant with state laws.
  • Build strong relationships with motivated sellers, buyers, and landlords.
  • Maintain emergency funds for vacancies or unexpected repairs.
