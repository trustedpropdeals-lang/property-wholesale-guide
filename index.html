Property Wholesaling & Rental Arbitrage

Your guide to building wealth through real estate without owning property.

What is Property Wholesaling?

Property wholesaling involves finding undervalued real estate and assigning the contract to another buyer for a fee.

  • You secure a property under contract below market value.
  • You assign your contract rights to an investor buyer.
  • You collect an assignment fee at closing without taking ownership.

Steps to Start Wholesaling

  • Conduct local market research.
  • Build a cash buyers list.
  • Find distressed properties and motivated sellers.
  • Negotiate contracts and include assignment clauses.
  • Assign the contract to a buyer and collect your fee.

What is Rental Arbitrage?

Rental arbitrage means leasing a property long-term and subletting it as a short-term rental to profit from the difference in rents.

  • You rent a property and furnish it for short-term guests.
  • You list the property on platforms like Airbnb to earn a higher nightly rate.
  • You profit on the spread between your long-term rent and short-term rental income.

Steps to Start Rental Arbitrage

  • Check local regulations and lease terms.
  • Select properties in high-demand short-term rental markets.
  • Negotiate with landlords for permission to sublet.
  • Calculate your profit margins including rent, furnishings and fees.
  • Launch your listing with professional photos and dynamic pricing.

Tips & Considerations

  • Understand state laws and licensing requirements for wholesaling.
  • Build strong relationships with buyers, sellers and landlords.
  • Keep reserves for vacancies, repairs and unforeseen expenses.
  • Start small and scale as you gain experience.
